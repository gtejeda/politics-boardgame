# Game Glossary

Consistent terminology is critical for a politically neutral game. This glossary defines terms as they are used in game design and play.

---

## Core Concepts

### Tradeoff
A design pattern where every benefit creates pressure elsewhere. No free gains exist.

### Delayed Effect
A policy outcome that manifests after one or more rounds, not immediately.

### Institutional Constraint
A rule or system that limits what actions are possible, regardless of player preference.

### Legitimacy
A resource representing public/institutional trust in the governing system.

### Stability
A measure of systemic resilience to shocks and pressures.

## Political Terms (Neutral Framing)

These terms are used without value judgment:

| Term | Game Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Left-leaning | Policies emphasizing collective welfare, regulation, redistribution | Toolkit, not identity |
| Right-leaning | Policies emphasizing markets, individual liberty, traditional institutions | Toolkit, not identity |
| Progressive | Policies favoring institutional change | Neutral descriptor |
| Conservative | Policies favoring institutional continuity | Neutral descriptor |

---

## Anti-Patterns (Terms to Avoid)

| Avoid | Use Instead | Reason |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| "Good policy" | "Effective under X conditions" | Implies moral judgment |
| "Bad outcome" | "Crisis state" or "Pressure threshold exceeded" | Neutral framing |
| "The right choice" | "Optimal for Y victory path" | Multiple paths exist |
